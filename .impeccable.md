## Design Context

### Users

This project is used by Carter and a book club of adult readers from varied professional backgrounds. For the `gamebook-viewer` experiment specifically, they are reading Neil Patrick Harris's _Choose Your Own Autobiography_ together and want a clear way to compare the paths they each took on a first read.

### Brand Personality

Organized, minimal, professional. The interface should feel calm and competent rather than playful or ornamental. It should support discussion and comparison without getting in the way.

### Aesthetic Direction

Light mode only. Editorial and structured rather than flashy: clean typography, strong information hierarchy, and a restrained presentation that still feels intentional. The viewer should work equally well on mobile and desktop, with layouts that adapt to reading, sharing, and comparing route strings.

### Design Principles

1. Make path comparison easy to scan at a glance.
2. Keep the interface minimal, but not sterile; every visual choice should improve legibility or orientation.
3. Treat the book and its branching structure with an editorial, reading-forward presentation.
4. Prioritize mobile and desktop usability equally; adapt the layout instead of hiding capability.
5. Use professional, stable interaction patterns that feel dependable for group discussion and sharing.
